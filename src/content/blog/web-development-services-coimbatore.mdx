## Types of Web Development Services in Coimbatore

Not all websites are the same. Understanding the differences helps you articulate what you actually need and avoid paying for what you do not.

**Business Websites** are the most common requirement. A standard business website includes your homepage, services or products page, about page, and contact page. It serves as your digital storefront — the place customers verify you are legitimate before calling or visiting. A good business website builds trust, communicates your unique value, and makes it easy for customers to contact you. These are best built with modern frameworks like Next.js for speed and SEO performance.

**Web Applications** are interactive software systems that run in a browser. Examples include booking platforms, customer portals, order management systems, inventory dashboards, and employee management tools. Web apps are significantly more complex and expensive than business websites — they involve user authentication, database operations, business logic, and often real-time data. If your project involves users logging in and doing things, you need a web application, not just a website.

**E-commerce Websites** enable online selling. A true e-commerce site includes product catalog management, shopping cart, payment gateway integration (Razorpay for India), order management, and GST invoice generation. For Indian businesses, GST compliance is non-negotiable — any e-commerce site must handle tax slabs correctly and generate proper invoices.

**Landing Pages** are single-page websites focused on a single goal: collecting a lead, promoting a product launch, or running a specific campaign. Landing pages are ideal for Google Ads or Meta Ads traffic — sending ad visitors to a focused page with one call-to-action converts far better than sending them to your homepage.

## What Technology Stack Should Your Website Use?

The technology your website is built on matters significantly — it affects speed, security, scalability, and long-term maintenance costs.

**Next.js vs WordPress: The Honest Comparison**

WordPress powers a large portion of websites worldwide and is the default choice for many local developers because it is fast to set up using templates. However, for serious businesses in 2025, Next.js is the superior choice in almost every dimension that matters.

WordPress advantages: Familiar CMS for non-technical users, massive plugin ecosystem, quick to set up with templates.

WordPress disadvantages: Plugin-heavy architecture leads to slow load times (2 to 5 seconds is common). Constant plugin updates and security patches. Plugin conflicts are frequent. Heavy resource consumption on servers. Template-based design limits customization without deep PHP knowledge.

Next.js advantages: Extremely fast load times (under 1 second is achievable). Excellent performance on Google's Core Web Vitals. No plugin vulnerabilities. Scales without degrading. Built-in image optimization. TypeScript support for code quality.

Next.js disadvantages: Requires a developer to make content changes (unless a headless CMS is added). Higher development cost upfront.

For a Coimbatore business that wants to rank on Google, impress customers, and not worry about security patches — Next.js is the right choice. At RYX Tech, every website we build uses Next.js.

**For web applications**, the stack matters even more. We recommend React and Next.js on the frontend, Python with Flask or FastAPI on the backend, and PostgreSQL for the database. This combination is battle-tested, well-supported, and long-term maintainable.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Web Developer in Coimbatore

Before signing with any web developer or agency in Coimbatore, ask these questions and evaluate the responses carefully:

**1. Can I see live examples of websites you have built?** Any serious developer has a portfolio. Visit the actual URLs, not screenshots. Check load speed using Google PageSpeed Insights.

**2. Who will be building my website — you or a subcontractor?** Many agencies outsource to cheaper developers. Know who is actually doing the work.

**3. What technology stack will you use?** If the answer is WordPress with a page builder, ask why and whether Next.js is an option. Understand what you are getting.

**4. What is included in the price?** Get a detailed scope in writing. SEO setup, contact form, WhatsApp integration, Google Analytics, and mobile responsiveness should be explicit line items — not assumptions.

**5. What happens after launch?** Is there a warranty period? What does post-launch support cost? Who do I contact if something breaks?

**6. Do I own the code and domain?** You should own 100% of your code, your domain, and your hosting account. Never give a developer control of your domain registrar.

**7. What is the payment structure?** A reasonable payment structure is 50% upfront and 50% upon delivery. Be cautious of developers asking for 100% upfront.
