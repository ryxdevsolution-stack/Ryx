## Beyond the Hype

ChatGPT has been live for over two years. The initial excitement has settled, and now we're seeing which use cases actually stick in real businesses versus which ones were just demos. This is a practical guide based on what Indian entrepreneurs are actually doing with it today.

## 1. Writing Client Proposals in Minutes

Before: A detailed project proposal took 3–4 hours to write from scratch.

With ChatGPT: You describe the client's problem, your solution, and the key points you want to make. ChatGPT drafts a structured proposal in 2 minutes. You spend 20 minutes editing and personalising. Total: 25 minutes.

**Prompt template:**
> "Write a project proposal for a [type of client] who needs [specific problem solved]. We are offering [your solution]. Include: executive summary, problem statement, proposed solution, timeline, and pricing section. Tone: professional but friendly."

## 2. Customer Support Scripts

Train your support team using ChatGPT-generated scripts that cover the most common questions. Better yet, embed a ChatGPT-powered chatbot on your website that handles tier-1 queries 24/7.

For Indian businesses, this is particularly powerful for after-hours WhatsApp queries that would otherwise wait until morning.

## 3. Social Media Content Calendar

Ask ChatGPT to generate 30 days of social media posts for your business — specific to your industry, product, and target audience. Spend 1 hour reviewing and editing. Schedule with Buffer or Hootsuite. Done.

**Time saved:** 6–8 hours/month

## 4. Market Research Summaries

Instead of reading 10 articles about a topic, paste them into ChatGPT and ask for a 500-word summary with key insights and implications for your business. The AI synthesises faster than any human researcher.

## 5. Job Descriptions and HR Templates

Writing a job description, offer letter, or employee handbook section used to require HR expertise or expensive consultants. ChatGPT produces professional drafts in minutes that your HR team or CA can review.

## 6. Email Follow-Up Sequences

For sales teams, ChatGPT can write entire email sequences: initial outreach → follow-up 1 → follow-up 2 → closing attempt → breakup email. Personalise the key details, and you have a repeatable sales engine.

## 7. Translating Business Content to Regional Languages

ChatGPT translates English content into Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, Malayalam, and other Indian languages with reasonable accuracy. For marketing materials targeting regional audiences, this is a starting point that a native speaker refines — not a perfect output.

**Important:** Always have a native speaker review before publishing.

## 8. Analysing Customer Feedback

Dump 100 customer reviews or support tickets into ChatGPT and ask: "Identify the top 5 recurring complaints and top 5 praise points. What are the most actionable improvements?"

You get a structured analysis in seconds instead of hours.

## 9. Creating Training Materials

Need to train a new employee on your billing process, customer handling protocol, or product range? Ask ChatGPT to convert your rough notes into a structured training guide with a quiz at the end.

## 10. Drafting Legal Notices and Agreements

For basic vendor agreements, NDAs, and service contracts, ChatGPT produces solid first drafts. This doesn't replace a lawyer for important contracts — but it reduces the legal fee by giving your lawyer a 80% complete document to review instead of starting from scratch.

**Cost saved:** ₹5,000–20,000 per document in legal fees.

## The Limits You Must Understand

- **ChatGPT can be confidently wrong** — always verify facts, especially numbers, dates, and legal details
- **It doesn't know your business** — the more context you give, the better the output
- **It's not a strategy** — it executes ideas, it doesn't generate them
- **Data privacy** — don't paste sensitive customer data or financial records into the public ChatGPT interface

## Getting the Most From ChatGPT

The quality of output is directly proportional to the quality of your prompt. Vague input produces vague output. Specific input — with context, examples, and constraints — produces work you can actually use.

Invest 30 minutes learning to write better prompts. It will pay back every day.
